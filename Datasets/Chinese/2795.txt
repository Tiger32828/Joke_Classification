Grizzly bears often haunt the forest east of the Alaska Cathedral.
Several charitable priests have printed pamphlets for travelers.
The manual recommends that travelers hang some small bells on their bodies when they go out in the wild, because the bells can scare away the nearby grizzly bears;
At the same time, it also reminds travelers that they should observe the road when walking in the wild, and be careful if they notice grizzly dung.
In addition, the manual also highlights a reminder to travelers that there is one condition that means a particularly dangerous grizzly bear is nearby:
"Small bells were found in bear droppings."
