A Marine Corps second lieutenant trained at the base as a platoon leader to command a battle-hardened platoon of soldiers on the main line of defense in Vietnam.
On the first night he arrived, his row was ordered to dig foxholes.
The hairy young man second lieutenant asked his non-commissioned officer, "Sergeant, where is my scatter pit?
"Second Lieutenant!" replied the sergeant. "You're standing in there, but you haven't dug out the dirt yet, sir."
