Once upon a time, there was a young master who spent his days eating, drinking, and loitering. He spent all the inheritance left by his father. As the end of the year approached, he didn't even have any firewood.
On New Year's Eve, the impoverished young master wrote a couplet of self-deprecating self-deprecation and posted it on the door: Do things frugally and live indifferently
After reading it, an old scholar in Murakami was so sighed that he added a word to each of the couplet's headers, which became: Do things thrift early to avoid indifference
